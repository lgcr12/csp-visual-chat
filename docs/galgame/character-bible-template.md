# Character Bible Template

The character bible protects route writing from drifting away from the source character. It should be generated or refined with the `csp` skill whenever possible.

## Template

```yaml
character: ""
work: ""
canon_window: ""
source_status: ""

core_identity:
  summary: ""
  public_role: ""
  private_pressure: ""
  central_contradiction: ""

world_constraints:
  timeline: ""
  allowed_locations: []
  blocked_locations: []
  knowledge_boundary: []
  social_constraints: []
  impossible_events: []

speech_dna:
  default_tone: ""
  sentence_shape: ""
  emotional_leakage: ""
  humor_style: ""
  avoidance_style: ""

  default_distance: ""

```

## Core Identity

This section answers why the character is recognizable.

It should include:

- What they protect.
- What they want but cannot easily admit.
- How they handle being seen.
- What makes them different from a generic romance character.

## World Constraints

This section prevents world drift.

It should include:

- Canon time period.
- Places that make sense.
- Places that do not.
- Information the character cannot know.
- Duties, social limits, or setting rules that remain active.

## Speech DNA

This section controls dialogue feel.

It should define:

- Sentence length.
- Formality.
- Self-reference.
- How they dodge.
- How they show affection.
- How they show anger.
- Words or tones that feel wrong.

## Relationship Logic

This section controls route pace.

It should define:

- What earns trust.
- What breaks trust.
- How fast intimacy can grow.
- Which behaviors are allowed at each relationship stage.
- What kind of closeness feels natural for the character.

## Stress Response

This section matters because routes often become unbelievable under pressure.

Define what the character does when:

- Embarrassed.
- Cornered.
- Comforted.
- Betrayed.
- Asked to be honest.
- Given too much affection too early.

## Route Hooks

Route hooks connect the character to the story structure.

Common route seed:

- A small situation that shows the character's ordinary defense.

Personal conflict:

- The deeper problem that can only open after trust.

Turning points:

- Events that change how the character sees the player.

Possible endings:

- End states that fit the character.

## Forbidden Actions

This is the most important practical section.

Examples:

- Cannot confess without prior trust.
- Cannot reveal a canon secret before the route supports it.
- Cannot abandon a duty for player convenience.
- Cannot speak in a tone that contradicts their established voice.

## OOC Risk Notes

List common ways this character can be written incorrectly.

Examples:

- Too soft too early.
- Too clingy without dependence being earned.
- Too direct when they normally deflect.
- Too knowledgeable about events outside their viewpoint.
